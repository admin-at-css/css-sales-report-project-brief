2. **Data aman dan tidak hilang** - Mau ganti HP atau HP rusak, data tetap ada (cloud backup)
3. **Bisa buat report meskipun tidak ada internet** - Offline mode critical! Sering di area tanpa signal
4. **Auto-save otomatis** - App simpan draft otomatis, tidak perlu khawatir data hilang kalau tiba-tiba ditelepon customer
5. **Mudah upload foto (1-3 foto per report)** - Quick photo attachment, no compression issues
6. **Bisa cek customer history** - Lihat reports lama untuk customer tertentu (kapan last visit, apa yang dibahas)
7. **Reminder to report** - Jangan sampai lupa report setelah visit

**Behaviors:**

- **Customer relationships matter** - Dia ingat customer lewat personal connections, not just data. App harus bantu dia maintain relationships.

**Quote:**
> "Saya lebih suka ketemu customer daripada ngisi-ngisi laporan. Tapi ya harus, buat bukti kita udah visit. Masalahnya, kalau tidak langsung tulis, sering lupa detail meeting. Males juga kalau harus ketik panjang-panjang di HP, apalagi setelah 4-5 visits."

## ðŸ“Š Persona Summary (Design Implications)

### For Sales Reps (Budi + Dina):
**Design untuk user yang:**
- âœ… **Mixed tech comfort** â†’ Design for lowest common denominator (Budi's basic tech level). Simple, clear UI. No jargon. Bahasa Indonesia labels.
- âœ… **Extremely time-constrained** â†’ 3-5 visits per day, always on-the-go. Target: **< 5 min per report** (Budi), **< 3 min** (Dina)
- âœ… **Interrupted frequently** â†’ Phone calls, WhatsApp messages, next appointment. **Auto-save drafts every 30s** is CRITICAL
- âœ… **Often offline** â†’ Pabrik, warehouse, basement proyek = no signal. **Offline-first architecture is NON-NEGOTIABLE**
- âœ… **Photo-dependent** â†’ Always take 1-3 photos per visit. Photo upload must be fast & easy
- âœ… **Diverse learning styles** â†’ Budi needs training + trial-error. Dina is quick learner. Design for both.

**Key Design Decisions:**

**1. Report Creation Flow:**
- **Draft-first workflow**: Budi can create quick draft after visit â†’ edit/complete later end-of-day
- **Large touch targets** (min 48dp) - Budi has older eyes, uses phone while tired
- **Clear, action-oriented labels** in Bahasa: "Buat Laporan Baru", "Simpan Draft", "Submit Laporan"
- **Minimal steps** - Don't make 10-step wizard. Single scrollable form preferred.
- **Smart defaults** - Pre-fill data when possible (GPS location, date/time, last product discussed with this customer)

**2. Auto-Save & Offline:**
- **Prominent auto-save indicator**: "Tersimpan otomatis 2 menit yang lalu" (Budi needs reassurance data is safe)
- **Clear offline mode**: Orange banner "Mode Offline - Data akan sync otomatis saat online" with sync icon
- **Sync status visible**: Show pending uploads count ("3 laporan menunggu upload")
- **Draft list**: Show all drafts with status badges (Draft, Pending Upload, Submitted)

**3. Photo Upload:**
- **Quick camera access** - Camera button prominent, one-tap to capture
- **1-3 photos per report** - Show photo slots (empty state: "Tambah Foto 1/3")
- **No compression issues** - Unlike WhatsApp, maintain photo quality
- **Offline photo handling** - Photos saved locally, auto-upload when online

**4. Customer History Access:**
- **"Lihat Riwayat" button** on report form - see past visits to this customer before writing new report
- **Simple list view** - Date, product discussed, outcome, photos
- **Search by customer name** - Autocomplete dropdown

**5. Notifications & Reminders:**
- **For Budi** (opt-in): "Jangan lupa buat laporan visit tadi!" (gentle reminder end-of-day)
- **For Dina** (more active): Progress notifications ("15 visits minggu ini, target 20!"), achievement badges
- **Smart timing**: Don't send notifications saat jam kerja sibuk (10 AM - 3 PM)

**6. Two-tiered Experience:**
- **Basic mode (default)**: Simple form, essential fields only. Perfect for Budi.
- **Advanced features** (optional): Templates, quick notes, target tracking for Dina. Don't force on Budi.

### For Manager (Rian):
**Design untuk user yang:**
- âœ… **Analytics-first, data-driven** â†’ Rian wants **insights**, not just reports. Show trends, patterns, actionable metrics.
- âœ… **Multi-device user** â†’ Phone (on-the-go quick checks), Laptop (deep analysis), Tablet (meetings). Responsive design critical.
- âœ… **Dashboard-first mindset** â†’ Overview cards/charts â†’ drill-down to individual reports. Don't show report list immediately.
- âœ… **Always online** â†’ Office WiFi + unlimited 4G. Offline mode nice-to-have, not critical.
- âœ… **Read-only** â†’ Manager CANNOT create/edit reports. Clear separation from sales rep role.
- âœ… **Filter-heavy user** â†’ Lives in filters: by date range, by sales rep, by customer, by product, by visit outcome.
- âœ… **Export-oriented** â†’ Needs to compile data untuk upper management (Excel/PDF export)

**Key Design Decisions:**

**1. Dashboard (Home Screen):**
**Top Priority: Analytics Cards**
- **"Visits Minggu Ini"**: 23 visits (â†‘ 15% vs last week) - with trend arrow
- **"Top 5 Customers"**: Bar chart showing most-visited customers this month
- **"Team Performance"**: Budi (12 visits), Dina (11 visits) - simple comparison
- **"Pending Follow-ups"**: 5 customers perlu follow-up (actionable insight)
- **"Conversion Rate"** (if tracked): X% visits jadi deals
- **Time-based view**: Quick filters "Hari Ini", "Minggu Ini", "Bulan Ini", "Custom Range"

**2. Reports List View (Secondary):**
- **After dashboard**, show filterable list of all reports
- **Prominent filters** (top of screen, always visible):
  - By Sales Rep (Budi, Dina, Semua)
  - By Date Range (date picker)
  - By Customer (searchable dropdown)
  - By Product Type (Paint, Tiles, Service Package)
- **Sorting options**: Newest first (default), Oldest first, By customer name, By sales rep
- **List item shows**: Customer name, Sales rep, Date, Visit outcome badge, Photo thumbnail (if any)

**3. Individual Report View (Drill-down):**
- **Read-only** - No edit/delete buttons (clearly differentiated from sales rep view)
- **All report details**: Customer, date, location (map?), products discussed, notes, photos
- **Customer history context**: "Last visit: 2 weeks ago" (quick context)
- **Export button**: "Export to PDF" for this individual report

**4. Multi-Device Optimization:**
- **Mobile (phone)**: **NO analytics dashboard** - too complex for small screen. Instead: Simple report list with basic filters (date, sales rep) + quick summary stats ("23 visits minggu ini"). Optimized for quick report viewing on-the-go.
- **Tablet**: Simplified dashboard - 2-3 key metric cards + basic charts. Hybrid between mobile and desktop.

**5. Export Functionality:**
- **Single report export**: PDF with company branding (CSS logo)
- **Bulk export**: "Export All Reports" â†’ Excel/CSV with all data fields (untuk upper management reports)

**6. No Create/Edit:**
- **No FAB button** (Floating Action Button) for "Create Report" - that's for sales reps only
- **No edit icons** on reports - view-only mode always
- **Clear role differentiation**: Manager dashboard looks completely different from sales rep home screen

**7. Scalability (5-10 reps coming):**
- **Performance tracking per rep** must scale (don't hardcode 2 reps)
- **Team comparison view**: Sortable table showing all reps' metrics side-by-side
- **Notification preferences**: As team grows, Rian may not want notif for EVERY report (opt-in for important only)

### Critical User Insights:

**Sales Reps (Primary Users):**
1. **Design for Budi (47, basic tech), not Dina (32, tech-savvy)** â†’ If Budi can use it, Dina will love it. If you design for Dina, Budi will struggle.
2. **OFFLINE-FIRST is NON-NEGOTIABLE** â†’ 80% of their time in warehouse/pabrik = no signal. App MUST work offline or it's useless.
3. **Auto-save every 30s or they lose data** â†’ Budi gets interrupted by calls/messages constantly. Draft data MUST survive interruptions.
4. **3-5 visits per day = exhausted users** â†’ By visit #4, Budi is tired. Keep UI simple, forgiving, fast.
5. **They FORGET details end-of-day** â†’ Enable draft-after-each-visit workflow. This is the #1 problem we're solving.
6. **Photos are easier than typing** â†’ 1-3 photos per report. Make camera access one-tap.
7. **One-handed use preferred** â†’ Often filling reports while holding coffee/bag/leaning on car. Bottom navigation, large touch targets.
8. **Bahasa Indonesia labels ONLY** â†’ "Buat Laporan", not "Create Report". Avoid English technical terms.

**Manager (Secondary User - Rian):**
1. **He's YOUNGER (31) than his senior sales rep (Budi, 47)** â†’ Modern, data-driven manager managing experienced team
2. **Analytics dashboard is DESKTOP-ONLY** â†’ Full analytics (charts, trends, insights) on laptop/desktop. Mobile = simple report list + basic stats only.
3. **Multi-device user with different needs per device** â†’ Phone (quick report viewing), Laptop (deep analytics), Tablet (meetings/presentations)
4. **Read-only ALWAYS** â†’ No create/edit/delete. Completely different UX from sales rep view.
5. **Filter-heavy user** â†’ He lives in filters. Make filtering fast, prominent, persistent (especially on desktop).
6. **Export is critical** â†’ He manually copy-pastes WhatsApp â†’ Excel now (5+ hrs/week). We must give him one-click export.
7. **Scalability matters** â†’ 2 reps now, 5-10 soon. Dashboard must scale, not hardcode 2 names.
